Service-oriented architectures (SOAs) are becoming more common, in part because they offer great flexibility: complex and dynamic patterns of interaction between services in different administrative domains are possible. These same characteristics pose challenges for ensuring system-wide security.

The goal of this project is to develop techniques and tools for managing, enforcing, and maintaining trust relationships in systems with service-oriented architectures. Our work is based on a broad conception of SOAs that allows services to interact across a variety of interfaces, in addition to XML-based interfaces that are commonly associated with SOAs. Consequently, our results are applicable to many legacy systems, as well as explicitly service-oriented systems, e.g., Web services.
